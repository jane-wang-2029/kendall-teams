How to apply

How to Apply

kTeams welcomes applications from international scale-ups, teams from universities and incubators, and economic development organizations. Individual entrepreneurs may also apply.

To begin the application process, please send an email inquiry to the organizers. The program operates on a rolling admission basis, and teams will receive confirmation or rejection within one week after submitting required materials.

Initial Contact: Email organizers@kendall-teams.com

Application Review: Confirmation or rejection within one week after materials received

Remote Preparation: Accepted teams participate in a remote preparation program

Contact Organizers

Application Process

  1. Initial Inquiry: Send email to organizers@kendall-teams.com expressing interest
  2. Application Materials: Submit required application materials (registration form and other documents as specified)
  3. Review Period: Application materials are reviewed by the organizers
  4. Notification: Receive confirmation or rejection within one week after materials are received
  5. Remote Preparation: Accepted teams participate in remote preparation program before the on-site experience
  6. Registration & Fees: Complete registration form and payment of applicable fees
  7. Program Participation: Engage in the 2-week on-site program in Boston

Note: Sponsors are encouraged. Teams sponsored by universities, incubators, or economic development organizations may have different application processes or fee structures.

Typical Team Profile

kTeams is designed for teams that meet the following criteria:

  • Company Stage: Incorporated for 4 years or less
  • Funding: Have received $1 million USD or less in institutional funding
  • Industry Focus: Preferably life sciences, though other high-technology sectors are welcome
  • Team Initiative: Demonstrated initiative and commitment to market expansion
  • Full Participation: Commitment to full participation including preparation activities
  • Additional Assets: Having revenues, awards, or other validation is helpful but not required

Teams should be prepared for intensive engagement, active networking, and dedicated work on market positioning and strategic development throughout the program.
