What kTeams Offers

kTeams is a 2-week hands-on US-market exploration program designed for international scale-ups. The program welcomes teams from universities, incubators, and economic development organizations. Individual entrepreneurs may also apply.

Participants engage directly with the Boston innovation ecosystem through structured activities, introductions, and immersive experiences designed to accelerate market entry and strategic positioning.

Why kTeams?

Key Benefits

  • Optimize Market Positioning: Refine your value proposition and market positioning strategies
  • Perfect Your Pitch: Develop and practice investor-ready presentations
  • Strategic Introductions: Connect with investors, partners, and potential customers in the Boston ecosystem
  • Ecosystem Access: Gain direct access to Kendall Square innovation community and CIC facilities
  • Network Building: Build relationships with mentors, speakers, and fellow entrepreneurs

kTeams is not a Boot Camp

Unlike traditional boot camps, kTeams focuses on high-touch, personalized market exploration rather than standardized curriculum. The program is designed as a "contact sport" where teams engage directly with the ecosystem, building real relationships and gaining practical insights tailored to their specific market entry challenges.

The curriculum emphasizes hands-on experience, strategic introductions, and personalized mentorship rather than classroom-based instruction. Each team receives customized guidance based on their industry, stage, and market goals.

Why Boston

Boston, particularly Kendall Square, represents one of the world's most concentrated innovation ecosystems. The region offers unparalleled access to:

  • Leading research universities (MIT, Harvard, and others)
  • World-class hospitals and research institutions
  • Venture capital firms and angel investors
  • Life sciences, biotech, and technology clusters
  • Corporate innovation labs and strategic partners
  • A supportive startup community and accelerator programs

kTeams leverages this ecosystem through strategic partnerships, including cooperation with CIC (Cambridge Innovation Center), providing participants with workspace, meeting facilities, and direct access to the Kendall Square innovation community during their stay.

kTeams Program

The kTeams program is an intensive 2-week experience designed to help international scale-ups understand, navigate, and engage with the US market. The program combines structured learning sessions, networking events, strategic introductions, and hands-on market exploration activities.

Throughout the program, teams work directly with experienced mentors and advisors to refine their market strategy, develop investor materials, and build relationships with key stakeholders in their target market.

kTeams Program Structure

The 2-week program is structured around several key components:

  • Orientation & Ecosystem Overview: Introduction to Boston innovation ecosystem and program expectations
  • Market Positioning Workshops: Sessions focused on refining value proposition and market strategy
  • Pitch Development: Guided development and practice of investor presentations
  • Strategic Introductions: Curated meetings with investors, partners, and potential customers
  • Site Visits: Tours of relevant companies, labs, and institutions
  • Networking Events: Opportunities to connect with entrepreneurs, mentors, and ecosystem players
  • Individual Consultations: One-on-one sessions with mentors and advisors
  • Program Wrap-up: Final presentations and action plan development

kTeams Extended Programs

In addition to the standard 2-week program, kTeams may offer extended program options tailored to specific needs of participants or partner organizations. These extended programs provide additional time for deeper market exploration, relationship building, and strategic development.

How to Apply

kTeams welcomes applications from international scale-ups, teams from universities and incubators, and economic development organizations. Individual entrepreneurs may also apply.

To begin the application process, please send an email inquiry to the organizers. The program operates on a rolling admission basis, and teams will receive confirmation or rejection within one week after submitting required materials.

Initial Contact: Email organizers@kendall-teams.com

Application Review: Confirmation or rejection within one week after materials received

Remote Preparation: Accepted teams participate in a remote preparation program

Application Process

  1. Initial Inquiry: Send email to organizers@kendall-teams.com expressing interest
  2. Application Materials: Submit required application materials (registration form and other documents as specified)
  3. Review Period: Application materials are reviewed by the organizers
  4. Notification: Receive confirmation or rejection within one week after materials are received
  5. Remote Preparation: Accepted teams participate in remote preparation program before the on-site experience
  6. Registration & Fees: Complete registration form and payment of applicable fees
  7. Program Participation: Engage in the 2-week on-site program in Boston

Note: Sponsors are encouraged. Teams sponsored by universities, incubators, or economic development organizations may have different application processes or fee structures.

Typical Team Profile

kTeams is designed for teams that meet the following criteria:

  • Company Stage: Incorporated for 4 years or less
  • Funding: Have received $1 million USD or less in institutional funding
  • Industry Focus: Preferably life sciences, though other high-technology sectors are welcome
  • Team Initiative: Demonstrated initiative and commitment to market expansion
  • Full Participation: Commitment to full participation including preparation activities
  • Additional Assets: Having revenues, awards, or other validation is helpful but not required

Teams should be prepared for intensive engagement, active networking, and dedicated work on market positioning and strategic development throughout the program.

History

kTeams has its roots in the eTeams@MIT program, which was hosted by MIT since 2011. The eTeams@MIT program established a proven model for helping international teams understand and engage with the US market through hands-on experience in the Boston innovation ecosystem.

kTeams spun off from eTeams@MIT with the same core team, many of the same speakers and mentors, and the same high-touch "contact-sport" approach to curriculum delivery. The program maintains the strong connections to MIT and the Greater Boston ecosystem that made eTeams@MIT successful, while offering flexibility and focus on Kendall Square as a hub for innovation.

The program leverages the same deep understanding of US startup culture, investor expectations, and market dynamics that were developed over years of running eTeams@MIT, ensuring participants receive proven, effective guidance in their market exploration journey.
